Momo's Recipe

To make Nepali momos, start by preparing the dough: mix all-purpose flour with a little salt and add water gradually until you form a smooth, firm dough, then let it rest for about 15–20 minutes. For the filling, you can use minced chicken or vegetables. For chicken momos, combine minced chicken with finely chopped onions, ginger-garlic paste, coriander, a little oil, salt, cumin, turmeric, and momo masala if you have it. For vegetarian momos, mix grated cabbage, grated carrots, chopped onions, ginger-garlic paste, coriander, oil, salt, pepper, and momo masala. After the dough has rested, divide it into small balls and roll each one into a thin circular wrapper. Place a spoonful of filling in the center and fold the wrapper into your preferred shape, like round pleated or half-moon.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Water
  • 250g minced chicken
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 tbsp garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp ginger paste
  • 2 tbsp fresh coriander (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • ½ tsp turmeric
  • ½ tsp cumin powder
  • ½ tsp momo masala (optional but gives that authentic taste)
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Prepare the Dough
    • Add flour + salt in a bowl.
    • Slowly add water and knead until smooth.
    • Cover and let it rest for 15–20 minutes.
  2. Prepare the Filling
    • Mix minced chicken, chopped onion, garlic, ginger, coriander, oil, turmeric, cumin, momo masala, and salt.
    • Combine well and keep in fridge for 10 minutes.
  3. Make the momos
    • Divide dough into small balls.
    • Roll each ball into a thin circle (about palm-sized).
    • Place 1 spoon of filling in the middle.
    • Fold and shape (half-moon or round pleated style).
  4. Steam the Momos
    • Grease your steamer tray to prevent sticking.
    • Place momos without touching each other.
    • Steam on medium heat for 10–12 minutes
    • When the wrapper turns shiny and firm, they are done.
